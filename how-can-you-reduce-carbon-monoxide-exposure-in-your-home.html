how Can You Reduce Carbon Monoxide Exposure in Your Home?

Carbon monoxide is a colorless, odorless and highly poisonous gas that results from the burning of any fuel source, including natural gas, oil and wood. While CO can be produced indoors from time to time, it’s mostly associated with outdoor activities like driving a car or running a generator. However, CO can also be produced indoors from faulty heating appliances or poorly installed gas appliances like water heaters. When carbon monoxide is produced indoors, it can be fatal if it isn’t removed from the air quickly enough. Fortunately, there are some things you can do to reduce CO exposure in your home. Let’s take a look at some of them:

Change your air filter regularly

CO is produced when fuel is burned, and it’s important to filter the air in your home to prevent it from leaking into the house. Air filters trap dust and other contaminants in the air so they don’t get sucked into your HVAC system and cause damage. Unfortunately, CO isn’t something air filters are designed to trap, so you’ll need to change your filter more often if you smell CO in your home. CO is a very small molecule, so it can easily pass through a filter that’s designed to trap larger contaminants like dust and pollen.Air filters are cheap and easy to replace, so it’s a good idea to change yours regularly.

Change your furnace filter regularly

Furnace filters are responsible for cleaning the air that enters your home. Unfortunately, they don’t trap CO like air filters do, so you’ll want to change yours more often if you smell CO in your home. Furnace filters trap other contaminants like dust and pollen, but CO is a very small molecule that can slip through. CO is produced as a byproduct of burning fuel, and it’s important to clean the air in your home. Furnace filters can be easily accessed and changed, so they’re an easy way to keep your home clean and safe.

Change your water heater filter regularly

Water heaters produce CO as a byproduct of burning fuel, so you’ll want to change your water heater filter regularly if you smell CO in your home. Install a CO detector in your home

CO detectors are used to detect the presence of CO and alert you when it’s present. CO detectors are cheap and easy to install, and they can be a great addition to your home. CO detectors are especially important in large homes with multiple floors, where CO can travel from one floor to another. If you have pets or children, it’s important to install a CO detector in their room as well. CO can be fatal if it isn’t removed from the air quickly enough, so it’s important to know when it’s in your home. CO detectors can be purchased at most hardware stores and are fairly simple to install.
